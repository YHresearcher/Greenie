The Sovereign Cashew:
Binh Phuoc's Heritage

Where ancient basalt red earth meets generational farming wisdom to produce the world's finest "Sovereign White Whole" cashew kernels.

The Alchemy of Basalt Red Earth

Binh Phuoc, Vietnam's "Cashew Capital," possesses a unique geological advantage. The deep, porous basalt red earth—remnants of ancient volcanic activity—provides an irreproducible mineral-rich foundation.

This soil retains perfect moisture during tropical monsoon rains while allowing critical root aeration. It is this precise mineral signature that imparts the Sovereign cashew with its signature density and complex fatty acid structure.

Our farmers are not merely laborers; they are stewards of this land with knowledge passed through four generations.

Technical Note

Mineral-rich basalt soil contributes to 15% higher oil content compared to coastal cashew varieties.

The Artisan Process

From the patient rhythm of sun-drying to the precision of hand-shelling, every kernel is handled with uncompromising respect.

I. Selective Harvesting

We harvest only when the cashew apple reaches peak ripeness, ensuring maximum kernel density and optimal flavor development.

II. Natural Sun-Drying

Elevated bamboo racks allow airflow to reduce moisture to a stable 5%, preserving the natural enzyme system intact.

III. Structural Grading

Each kernel is hand-peeled and sorted by weight. Only the "giants"—WW180 and WW210—are selected for the Sovereign line.
